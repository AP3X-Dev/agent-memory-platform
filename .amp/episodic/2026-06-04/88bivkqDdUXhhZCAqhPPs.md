HVAC contractor operational research for grounding AG3NT/Hermes agentic scenarios.

FIELD-SERVICE/CRM SOFTWARE (real products): ServiceTitan (mid/large, dominant enterprise FSM, 100k+ contractors), Housecall Pro (SMB residential, easiest UI), Jobber (SMB residential), FieldEdge (best QuickBooks integration, strong service-agreement automation), BuildOps (commercial, PM-contract heavy), ServiceTrade (commercial service contractors), Workiz. QuickBooks is the back-office accounting layer most integrate with.

NO-API / PORTAL-DRIVEN SYSTEMS (the bot-protected, login-gated systems agents must navigate):
- Warranty claims: ServiceBench (by Asurion) is the dominant third-party warranty claim portal used across Carrier/Bryant/Payne and many brands. Manufacturer dealer portals: Carrier HVACpartners (carrier.hvacpartners.com), Trane ComfortSite, Lennox LennoxPros/LennoxPro, Goodman/Daikin product registration apps. Warranty REGISTRATION must happen within 60-90 days of install or coverage drops to base (e.g. Goodman 5yr base vs 10yr registered).
- Distributor/supply-house ordering portals (Watsco family): CarrierEnterprise.com, Gemaire.com, BakerDist.com (+ HVAC Pro+ mobile app), ECMDI.com (East Coast Metal). Also Ferguson.com, Trane Supply, Lennox Stores. Will-call/same-day pickup is core workflow.
- Permit/inspection portals: Accela (aca-prod.accela.com/<jurisdiction>) and eTRAKiT are the two dominant municipal platforms; thousands of city/county variants. Mechanical permit required for installs, licensed contractor pulls it.
- Utility rebate/incentive portals: DSIRE (dsireusa.org) and ENERGY STAR Rebate Finder are the lookup aggregators; each utility (e.g. Oncor) runs its own participating-contractor portal. Programs cap out early in funding year. Midstream/instant rebates applied at point of sale by registered contractor.
- Consumer financing portals: GreenSky, Synchrony, Wells Fargo, Service Finance Company, Microf. Contractor runs application at point of sale for 0%-promo financing.

REVENUE WORKFLOWS: lead -> booked call -> dispatch -> diagnostic -> flat-rate-book pricing -> Good/Better/Best proposal -> close -> install -> mobile invoice -> collect. Maintenance/membership agreements = 30-55% of revenue, smooth seasonality. Replacement/install jobs are highest ticket.

MONEY LEAKS: missed inbound calls (avg 27%, 35%+ in peak = $45k-120k/yr lost for $0.5-2M shops); unbilled service calls/parts (10% leak); slow diagnostic-to-proposal turnaround loses jobs; unfiled/denied warranty claims (parts must return within 30 days of claim or full denial; labor reimbursed at manufacturer rate < actual). AHRI matchup certificate (ahridirectory.org) required for rebates/efficiency claims.

PAIN POINTS 2025-26: A2L refrigerant transition (R-454B/R-32 replacing R-410A; cylinder shortage, prices $345->$2000+, 300%+ spike); technician labor shortage (110k+ gap, aging workforce, projected 300k shortfall by 2031); material cost inflation (steel/copper +40%, tariffs); AR collection; callbacks.

SEASONALITY: summer cooling peak = busiest/most profitable; winter heating secondary peak; shoulder seasons (spring/fall) slow -> contractors push maintenance agreements and tune-ups to fill schedules.
